I am a researcher working on computer vision, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. My goal is to build a machine that can "perceive" the world, "understand" what it sees, and "learn" new concepts from interacting with the environment. I believe that "simplicity is the ultimate sophistication". Such a machine can only be built with the belief. I have been marching toward my goal for almost a decade and have explored several important problems in the field including semantic scene labeling, object detection, image segmentation, object pose estimation, shape matching, and image upsampling. I am currently employed by the Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories (MERL), Cambridge, MA. A copy of my resume can be download via the link.
